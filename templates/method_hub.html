Prop Bets

A prop bet is a wager on a specific player, team, or game event instead of the final winner. Common examples are points, rebounds, hits, strikeouts, passing yards, touchdowns, or whether a player clears a listed line.
Props are useful because they isolate one part of the matchup. The job is not just finding an over or under; it is deciding whether the line, role, matchup, market, and recent form are all telling the same story.
Player PropsPoints, rebounds, assists, hits, strikeouts, passing yards, receptions, touchdowns.
Game PropsTeam totals, first score, alternate totals, race-to markets, or game event outcomes.
Over / UnderOver means the player clears the line. Under means the player stays below it.
Yes / NoEvent props like touchdown, home run, double-double, steal, block, or recorded hit.

Market Edge is the pricing layer. It asks whether the current number is still worth attacking after line movement, book disagreement, implied odds, and matchup context are checked.
A good player read can become a pass if the market has already moved. Start with the sport, then compare price, line, role, and timing before deciding whether the edge is real.
Line MovementWatch whether the number moved toward or away from the model side.
Book SplitCompare prices across books instead of trusting one stale number.
Implied PriceConvert odds into probability so the read can be judged against cost.
Timing RiskLate injury news, starters, weather, and limits can change the market fast.

A parlay combines multiple picks into one ticket. The payout rises because every leg has to win, so the workflow needs correlation, bankroll discipline, and cleaner leg quality than a single bet.
The goal is not to stack every favorite. Start with a sport, choose legs that make sense together, avoid duplicated risk, and keep the ticket size aligned with the bankroll.
Leg QualityEach pick should stand on its own before it belongs in a ticket.
CorrelationIQCheck whether legs support each other or fight the same game script.
BankrollIQSize the ticket by risk instead of chasing the biggest payout.
Ticket ShapeThree to five cleaner legs often beats a long lottery build.
